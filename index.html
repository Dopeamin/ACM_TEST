About Us

ACM

ACM INSAT

AWARDS

ACM, the Association for Computing Machinery is the world's largest scientific and educational computing society. It is an international organization for academic and scholarly interests in computer science, claiming nearly 100,000 student and professional members as of 2019. And it is organized into over 171 local chapters and 37 Special Interest Groups (SIGs), through which it conducts most of its activities.

It all started with a contest and a group of ambitious students. On the 26th of February 2017, the 1st edition of INSAT CPC was held. A new experience, an unknown territory, and yet 33 teams of INSAT students have registered, thus marking a record of the university with the most participating teams in Tunisia.
After displaying such performance, the club was founded as “ACM INSAT” on the 12th of April 2017 with more than 200 members signing in from the start, and an INFO session was organised to introduce the club and present its two main goals, which remain the same as the Student Chapter was officialy chartered by ACM in July 2018.
The chapter organizes various activities such as technical talks, workshops, programming contests and many others. Participating enhances both student’s soft skills and technical skills in computing. the chapter also provides a natural setting to develop and demonstrate the leadership capabilities critical to students’ career development.

Activities

HOUR OF CODE

Hour of Code is a global initiative that aims to teach children, mainly primary school students, some aspect of programming within one Hour. For this year, we visited a center for children who live in harsh family environnement. Our goal was to show to these disadvantaged kids the powers of software and inspire them to take up coding.

DATA OVERFLOW

The Data Overflow project is a collaboration between ACM INSAT Student Chapter and IEEE INSAT Computer Society Chapter.
It is a program that consists of weekly training sessions held in the National Institute of Applied Science and Technology welcoming anyone interested in Data Science. Through these open doors, we hope to reach the biggest number of students and create a wide community that exchanges knowledge and experience in Data Science and related fields.
The project will be concluded with a three-day conference featuring a 48-hour entrepreneurship Bootcamp for AI oriented solutions , and a 24-hour technical AI hackathon.
Visit DataOverflow website now !!

THE NEW GENERATION PROGRAM

The New Generation Program aims to train the next generation of competitive problem solvers. Our goal is to qualify for the ICPC, and so far we managed to obtain many first places nationally and regionally. We aim with this program to raise champions of the same caliber.

Contests

WinterCup

The Winter Cup is a competition that revolves around problem solving abilities and advanced algorithms knowledge. It is a teams competition where each team have to solve more algorithmic problems faster, using C/C++ or Java!

SheSolves

SheSolves is a cross university programming competition initiated in 2019 by INSAT ACM STUDENT CHAPTER and IEEE WIE INSAT Student Affinity Group in the purpose of introducing female university and high school students to competitive programming and encouraging them to participate in bigger regional and nation wide contests and improve their problem solving skills. This year SheSolves will be on the 26th of January 2020 at INSAT (National Institute of Applied Science and Technology).
